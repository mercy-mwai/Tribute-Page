His Excellency Mwai Kibaki

His Excellency Mwai Kibaki with His Excellency Daniel Moi and other political leaders outside state house in 1979

Here is the time line of Mwai KIbaki's life:

  • 1931- Born in Othaya,Kenya.
  • 1955- He attended Makerere University in Uganda.
  • 1959-He attended the London School of Business.Hethen worked as a teacher before becoming active in the Kenyan struggle for Independence from The Great Britain.
  • 1963-After Kenya became an independent country he won a seat in National Assembly as a member of KANU.
  • 1969-1982-He served as the minister of finance and vice president in (1978-1988).
  • 1992-He challenged the then president ,president Moi, in the general elections and officially became the leader of opposition upto 1997.
  • 2002-He was elected as the third president of Kenya.As president, Kibaki pledged to eliminate the government corruption that had ruined the country's economy and had resulted in the withdrawal of foreign aid
  • 2005-A new constitution, backed by Kibaki, was finally put to referendum in November 2005, but it was rejected by voters; the rejection was viewed by many as a public indictment of Kibaki's administration
  • 2007-In preparation for the general elections he formed a new coalition party PNU.After delay release of results he was announced as the winner of the presidential seat.
  • 2008-Kibaki and Odinga signed a power sharing plan brokered by the late Koffi Annan who was the UN-secretary general and the former president of Tanzania Jakaya kikwete
  • 2010-The new constitution was approved by voters in a referendum and he signed it into law on August 27th
  • 2013-Kibaki stepped down at the end of his term in April 2013 and was succeeded by Kenyatta.
  • 2016-He lost his spouse ,Lucy Kibaki, after a brief hospitalization for chest pains at the age of 80
  • 2022 -His death was announced by the then president,Uhuru, who issued a proclamation that Kibaki would be granted a state funeral with full cibilian and millitary honors.

"His greatest moment was the promulgation of the new Constitution... It was a very deep and emotional moment for him," Kibaki's son Jimmy was quoted as saying
